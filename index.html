About me

Hi! I'm Francis and welcome to my portfolio website.

I created this website to showcase my passion which is web development. This website will also act as a receipt of my journey into becoming a real front-end developer. I'm 30 years old, quit my 8-5 office job to pursue my dream of being a developer, and now in the hunt for companies that would help turn this dream into a reality. Everything I learned so far is self-taught. I would like to thank YouTube, Udemy, Scrimba, and the Odin Project for providing me the sources that I need. I hope someday that I'll be able to put the title "Developer" besides my name. Some may call my decisions stupid, but I'll call it a leap of faith.

I would like to thank my cousin for teaching me on how to use the computer (ISP Bonanza era). He ignited my passion of playing computer games, I've spent half of my childhood in front of the computer playing, and was already playing with guys thrice my age competitively. No matter what game it is, I always push myself to be good at it. Being always in front of the computer opened a lot of opportunities for me. I discovered the power of being a member of forums where I learned a lot of things, from repairing and modding phones, to using VPNs to access the web for free, and much more. I was already repairing computers and phones when I was in high school, and because of this, it helped me survive high school without relying so much to my parents. What pushed me into front-end development was when Friendster was created. I found out that you could modify your bland profile page to a dynamic one. This made me a member of a modding group that teaches aspiring modders how to bedazzle their friend by modifying their profile page.

As I have mentioned earlier, everything here is self-taught. It would be a long list if I decide to give a shoutout to every person, YouTube channel, discord user, reddit users, and websites that helped me hone the skills that I have right now. Don't worry, this list will not stop growing on par of my drive to continue improving my craft. I love creating websites using vanilla HTML, CSS and Javascript. But lately, I've been having fun using TailwindCSS while studying React. I am now confident that I have what it takes to be a front-end developer.
